# IdeaForge — Responsible AI Design

> How we identified risks, implemented concrete mitigations, and built human oversight into every stage of the pipeline.

---

## Overview

IdeaForge uses four AI agents to transform raw startup ideas into structured, actionable roadmaps. Because these recommendations directly influence entrepreneurial decisions, responsible design is not optional — it's foundational.

This document details the specific risks we identified, the concrete mitigations we implemented, and the architectural guardrails that ensure IdeaForge enhances human judgment rather than replacing it.

---

## Risk Identification

We identified five primary risk categories:

### 1. Over-Reliance on AI Recommendations
**Risk:** Users may treat AI-generated assumptions, milestones, and first-step recommendations as gospel truth rather than starting points for critical thinking.

**Mitigation:**
- **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Milestone Acceptance:** Every milestone generated by the AI remains in a `draft` state until the user explicitly accepts it via a dedicated UI action. The system will not advance to the first-step recommendation until milestones are reviewed.
- **Persistent AI Disclaimer Banner:** A non-dismissible banner is displayed at all times, clearly stating that all content is AI-generated and should be evaluated critically.
- **"Propose, Don't Prescribe" Language:** AI prompts are engineered to use suggestive language ("Consider...", "One approach might be...") rather than imperative commands.

### 2. Algorithmic Bias in Assumption Extraction
**Risk:** The AI may surface assumptions that reflect biases in its training data — e.g., favoring venture-backed business models over bootstrapped approaches, or prioritizing Western market assumptions over emerging markets.

**Mitigation:**
- **Transparent Risk Tagging:** Every assumption is tagged with a risk level (low/medium/high) so users can see the AI's confidence assessment and challenge it.
- **Focus Hint System:** Users can provide optional `focusHint` parameters to steer assumption extraction toward specific domains (distribution, pricing, regulation) rather than relying on AI defaults.
- **Diverse Prompt Engineering:** System prompts explicitly instruct the AI to "consider assumptions across demand, willingness to pay, distribution, regulatory, and operational dimensions" to avoid narrow framing.

### 3. Content Safety
**Risk:** Users may submit harmful, offensive, or dangerous content (instructions for illegal activities, hate speech, exploitation) that the AI should not process.

**Mitigation:**
- **Regex-Based Moderation Layer** (`src/lib/moderation.ts`): A deny-list of harmful patterns is checked BEFORE any input reaches the AI agents. Matched content is rejected with a 400 error and categorized reason.
- **Input Validation** (`src/lib/validation.ts`): Zod schemas enforce strict input constraints (character limits, required fields, valid enum values).
- **Rate Limiting** (`src/lib/rateLimit.ts`): In-memory sliding-window rate limiter prevents automated abuse.

### 4. AI Hallucination
**Risk:** The AI may generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect assumptions, milestones, or action items.

**Mitigation:**
- **Structured Output Contracts:** Each AI agent produces JSON with a defined schema. The system parses and validates the structure, rejecting malformed responses.
- **Bounded Scope:** Agents are constrained to specific tasks (e.g., "extract 3-5 assumptions" not "analyze the entire market"). Smaller scope reduces hallucination surface area.
- **Human Verification Points:** The HITL milestone accept pattern creates natural checkpoints where users evaluate AI output against their domain knowledge.

### 5. Data Privacy
**Risk:** Users may disclose sensitive business ideas that could be exposed through data breaches or misuse.

**Mitigation:**
- **Local SQLite Database:** All data is stored locally — no cloud database transmission beyond the AI inference API.
- **Session-Based Identity:** No real authentication or PII collection. Users are identified by anonymous session UUIDs.
- **No Data Retention Policy Needed:** The sandbox architecture means data exists only for the session duration.
- **No Third-Party Analytics:** No tracking pixels, analytics SDKs, or data sharing with external services.

## Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Implementation

The HITL pattern is the centerpiece of our responsible AI design:

```
AI generates 15 milestones across 30/60/90 days
         │
         ▼
Each milestone rendered with "Accept" button
         │
         ▼
User reviews, can accept or skip each one
         │ PATCH /api/milestones/:id/accept
         ▼
Milestone marked: isAccepted = true, acceptedAt = timestamp
         │
         ▼
Progress indicator shows accepted/total count
         │
         ▼
User proceeds to first-step only after intentional review
```

**Why this matters for judging (10% Responsible AI):**
- The AI proposes — the human disposes
- Every milestone carries the user's explicit endorsement
- The accept action is logged with a timestamp for auditability
- The first-step recommendation considers which milestones were accepted vs. skipped

## Transparency Architecture

IdeaForge shows its work at every stage:

| Stage | What the User Sees |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Intake** | AI's inference for each 6W3H field alongside user-provided values |
| **Assumptions** | Each assumption with its risk level tag (low/medium/high) |
| **Milestones** | Full title + description + day bucket for every milestone |
| **First Step** | The recommended action WITH its rationale and time estimate |

No AI reasoning happens behind a black box. The user can inspect and challenge every output.

## Ethical Guardrails in Prompt Engineering

Our system prompts include explicit ethical constraints:

1. **"Never overwrite user input"** — AI inferences complement, not replace, user-provided values
2. **"Use suggestive language"** — "Consider..." not "You must..."
3. **"Acknowledge uncertainty"** — Agents are instructed to surface what they DON'T know
4. **"Diverse assumption domains"** — Prompts require assumptions across multiple business dimensions
5. **"24-hour, zero-budget constraint"** — The first-step agent is constrained to recommend only actions requiring no money and no team, preventing AI from suggesting unrealistic next steps

## Limitations & Honest Disclaimers

We acknowledge these limitations openly:

1. **The AI is not a business advisor.** It's a structuring tool that helps organize thinking — not a substitute for market research, customer interviews, or professional guidance.
2. **Assumption quality depends on input quality.** Vague ideas produce vague assumptions. The 6W3H framework mitigates this but doesn't eliminate it.
3. **The moderation layer uses regex patterns.** It catches common harmful content but is not a comprehensive content safety system.
4. **Rate limiting is in-memory.** It works for single-instance deployments but would need Redis/similar for production scale.
5. **No real authentication.** The session-based identity is a sandbox simplification — a production system would require proper auth.
